The Experience

A house of music, fire,
and Moroccan grace.

In the 9th century, the polymath Ziryab carried the songs of Baghdad to the courts of Andalusia — refining table, dress, and music into a single ritual. Today, in Rabat, Le Ziryab honors his legacy.

Our salon is a stage. An Andalusian trio plays Oud and Qanun nightly. Our kitchen draws from the imperial cities of Fes, Marrakech, and Tetouan, and the citrus gardens of old Granada.

"To dine here is to enter a symphony — composed of flame, spice, and the slow rhythm of an Andalusian night."
— Chef Hicham & the House of Ziryab
